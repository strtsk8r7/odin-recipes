Pesto Bean And Rice Soup

Description

This hearty soup has become a favorite winter time dinner. Inspired originally from Delish I have adapted it into a cajun style soup reminiscent of Gumbo.

  • Ingredients
  • For Pesto
  • 1/2 c. Toasted Pine Nuts
  • 2 tbsp Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 2 Clove Garlic
  • 1/4 tsp Sea Salt
  • Freshly Ground Black Pepper
  • 2 c. Basil Leaves
  • 1/4 c. Extra-Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1/4 c. Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • For Soup
  • 2 Slices Thick Cut Bacon, diced
  • 1/2 lb Andouille Sausage
  • 1 Small Yellow Onion, diced
  • 2 Medium Carrots, diced
  • 2 Ribs Celery, diced
  • 4 Cloves Garlic, diced
  • 1/4 c. Sundried Tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp Kosher Salt
  • 1 tsp Dried Oregano
  • 1/4 tsp. Red Pepper Flakes (optional)
  • 1/2 c. Short-Grain White Rice, rinsed
  • 4 c. Low-Sodium Chicken Broth, divided
  • 2 15-oz Can White Beans
  • 1/2 c. Pesto
  • 2 tbsp Parsley, chopped
  • Freshly Grated Parmesan, for serving

Directions

Making the Pesto

  1. In a food processor, combine the pine nuts, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper and pulse until well chopped.
  2. Add the basil and pulse until combined.
  3. With the food processor running, drizzle in the olive oil and pulse until combined. Add the Parmesan cheese, if using, and pulse to briefly combine. For a smoother pesto, add more olive oil.
  4. Reserve 1/2 c. Pesto for the soup and place to remainder into ice cube tray & freeze for future use.

Making the Soup

  1. In a large stockpt cook the bacon & andouille over medium heat until the bacon is browned.

    While the meat is cooking add the rice and 1/2 cup of chicken stock to a pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 3 minutes, followed by a natural pressure release of 10 minutes. (See note 1 if you do not have a pressure cooker)

    Remove the bacon and sausage from the pot with a slotted spoon, onto a plate, leaving the grease.

  2. Add the onion, carrots, & celery to the pot and sautee for about 5 minutes until they are softened.
  3. Once the vegetables have softened add the garlic & sundried tomatoes and cook for 30 seconds.
  4. Add the remaining 3 1/2 cups of broth, salt, oregano, & red pepper, bring to a simmer, stirring occasionally. Cover and reduce heat to medium-low. Cook for 20 minutes.
  5. While the vegetables are cooking dice the andouille into bite sized pieces
  6. Return the meat to the pot, stir in the rice & beans and simmer for an additional 12 minutes or until rice is completely tender.
  7. Remove from heat and stir in the pesto & parsley.
  8. Pour into bowls and top with freshly grated parmesan.

Notes

  1. The rice can be cooked directly in the pot with the vegetables. After step 3 add the rice to the pot and toast for 2 minutes.

    Add 2 cups of broth to the pot, the remaining ingredients from step 4, bring to a simmer, cover and reduce heat to medium low for 30-35 minutes until rice is al dente.

    Add the remaining 2 cups of broth to the pot, return the heat to medium, and proceed with step 5.
